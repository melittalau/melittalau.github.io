Event-Microsite for Classic Open Air

The “Classic Open Air” festival celebrates classical music and aims to make it accessible to a broad audience. This project’s goal was to create a website that reflects the event’s exciting spirit and makes it easy to purchase tickets.

Role

Brand Designer, UX/UI Designer

Background

This project was part of my IRONHACK UX/UI course. The prompt was to design an event microsite within one week with a focus mainly on UI Design, complex prototyping and animation.

About the Festival and its Audience

Every summer, the historic Gendarmenmarkt in Berlin hosts the "Classic Open Air" Festival, a five-day event that features a variety of classical music styles supported by light shows and fireworks.
Typically, classical music is enjoyed mostly by an older demographic (as seen in the chart below) and has an image of being stuffy, elite, or even inaccessible. However, the "Classic Open Air" Festival aims to make it more appealing to a general audience by inviting well-known pop artists to perform alongside the orchestra and by playing recognizable classics that everyone can enjoy.

Source: https://classicopenair.de/presse/

As a result, the festival attracts guests who may not typically attend classical concerts or the opera. According to the ‘Classic Open Air’ website, 70% of visitors do not otherwise attend any classical music events.
Following the target audience, these might be personas that reflect the festival goers.

Status Quo of the Website and Objectives

The festival celebrates classical music and makes it more accessible to a wide audience. Unfortunately the current website does not reflect this mission. A short analysis reveals an outdated, non-responsive design, confusing navigation and difficult-to-access ticket-purchasing.

To rework the website, the most important objectives are to:

  • Create a design that visually reflects the excitement of the event and the magic of classical music.

  • Make ticket purchasing more prominent and accessible.

  • Bring the website up to modern design standards, remove unnecessary features and information, and make it accessible, easy to navigate, and enjoyable to visit.

Most of the advertising and ticket purchasing is currently happening offline. By creating a better digital presence, the festival can cater to the needs of every demographic in its target group.

Rebranding

The design of the brand should reflect the festival itself. It should be elegant and timeless while also conveying the emotions that music evokes.

The existing colour palette of rich gold accents and dark base colours is a great foundation to resemble the ambience of a festival at night, with stage lights and fireworks. Adding another salmon tone makes the palette more organic. The well-readable Libre Franklin for paragraphs mixes harmoneously with an elegant, timeless classical serif font for display titles.

The logo should be recognizable, readable, and scalable while speaking a language of elegance. A high-contrast font with a rhythm of interchanging thick and thin lines reminds one of music or dancing and is the perfect fit for a classical event.

Designing a new website

My first layout approach reflected the existing sitemap of the website and consisted of a homepage and landing pages for the program, festival information and ticket purchasing. However, upon testing the wireframe with a couple of my peers, the overall feedback was that the site was still quite complex and had duplicate information.
The second wireframe, a one-page experience received more positive feedback. It was perceived as providing a better overview and having all information in one place. The one-page format also provided a more explorative, entertaining feel.

The flow of the website follows the AIDA principle:
A - A video in the hero banner captures the user’s attention and sparks curiosity.
I - As the user scrolls, they can satisfy their interest by discovering the acts and artists.
D - This creates a desire to attend the event, which can be further reinforced by an FAQ section that eliminates any doubts visitors might have. The transparency of the information provided also fosters a sense of trust.
A - Finally, a large banner calls for action to buy a ticket.

On mobile, the website follows the same flow, with the addition of a classic hamburger menu.

Conclusion

The new design fulfills the goals of the personas Sabine and Mario to purchase tickets, discover the program and find information about the festival. Large, emotional images and a dark, elegant colour palette visually reflect the feel of the event and of classical music itself. Ticket purchasing is now omnipresent in the header and throughout the landing page and will boost online ticket sales. Lastly, the new website is responsive and up to modern design standards. Unnecessary features and information are removed.

It will be exciting to observe how the digital presentation of the event evolves in the years to come, both in terms of the website and social media presence.
