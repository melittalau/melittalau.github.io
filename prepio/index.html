Prepio Web-App

Organising rehearsals for large groups is challenging. ‘Preprio’ helps team members stay on top of their tasks and appointments and helps organisers plan and create transparency.

Role

Brand Designer, UX/UI Designer & Researcher

Background

I created this web-app for my final project at the IRONHACK UX/UI course. The prompt was to create a digital product within two weeks, guided by the Design Thinking process.

Born from personal experience: Frustrations when planning rehearsals

I love creating and performing theatre-pieces. I love the teamwork and the thrill of making the audience laugh, cry and feel entertained. Shows are complex to plan and develop and require a lot of organisational overhead, it can be challenging, even with an amazing team.
When everybody involved is very passionate and invests much of their spare time, it can also create conflict. Some people might drop out at the last minute before a rehearsal or not turn up without notice. There can be too many channels used for communication and a lack of transparency on the agenda. Such difficulties can have a great impact on everybodies motivation.

I wanted to find out, if other groups are faced with similar problems and create a solution.

Research, Empathizing, Defining the Problem

After running a survey, I found some of my frustrations where quite common, but there were also other problems.

  • 85% of people feel like they need to be better prepared for rehearsal and know what to prepare exactly.

  • 65% identify the biggest obstacle to be people not attending.

  • 50% get frustrated with different levels of commitment of other members.

  • 50% feel lost due to an overwhelming amount of different information on different channels.

Excerpts from survey run with Typeform

In the following interviews I wanted to know: Why are people not attending? How does that make you feel? What are the consequences for the group and the project? How do you prepare your rehearsal? How successful is it?

The interviews revealed that most problems were more relevant for larger groups that don’t have a supervisor and organise themselves rather democratically. In these groups, the dynamics are much different, with more focus on personal commitment and relationships, which can a great impact on the individual motivation.
It seemed, what might help groups overcome their challenges, is to have a clear, shared goal. It also appeared an increate in transparency can reduce friction.

The focus was on three topic:

  • How might we create more transparency about who is attending?

  • How might we keep up the motivation & commitment?

  • How might we create an easy overview on all relevant information to keep everyone on the same page?

Three personas reflect the different personalities that contribute most to the group-dynamics. They reflect the participants found in surveys and interviews.

Breakdown of 3 Personas

  • Naomi, the exhausted organiser, needs an easy way to keep track of who will be attending the rehearsal, so she can plan the session most effectively.


  • Julie, the busy student member, needs to know what to practise, in case she can not attend. She also needs an easy way to let people know, if she can not make it.


  • Phil, the ambitious idea-powerhouse, needs more transparency to know what he can effectively contribute to the team because he does not want to waste his energy.

Designing solutions, Branding & Usertesting

Organising rehearsals for large groups is challenging. ‘Preprio’ helps team members stay on top of their tasks and appointments and helps organisers plan and create transparency.

Key Hypothesis Statement

When digging into why people are not attending, I found that in 3/4 of all cases, their work, school projects or illness get in the way. Life happens and some things can not be prevented. So I made it my commitment to keep members on track with what is happening in the group when they can not attend and to give them a chance to still contribute to the projects.

I soon found that, due to their busy lives, Naomi, Julie and Phil most likely need a solution on their phone, for when they are on the train, at break of work or uni or at rehearsal. The product would be an app.

Collection of UX-Methods used: drafted userflow, sidemap, idea-sketches, empathy-notes and UX canvas

They say: “Fail fast!”, so I created a paper prototype to test the ideas and the userflow for the persona Julie.

The task was to sign off from the upcoming rehearsal, find out what you will be missing and then add feedback to rehearsal from last week. 

After testing I translated my observations and findings into a digital low-fidelity prototype and repeated the usertest using the ‘Marvel’-App. It provided a great chance to see how users would interact with their phonesand helped optimise the usability early on, and finding the correct size for fonts, buttons and other elements.

Paper Prototype & Low-Fidelity digital Prototype

Turning my attention to branding, the first challenge was to find a suitable, available name for the brand. I asked my piers to vote from a selection of ideas and with an overwhelming majority, “Prepio” was chosen as a name for the product.

I wanted to create a brand that is fun and encouraging but at the same time conveyed a feeling of transparency and organisation.

Moodboard

The text in the app had to be easily readable, so I opted for Inter for paragraphs and the playful, more organic Livvic for headlines. For the colour-palettes, the blue palette provided a versatile foundation with a clean and organised feel to it, while red and green tones add a more lively touch.

Components

When creating the final designs, I found myself going back to my personas and their problems constantly. Would this help them? Would they want to use this app to begin with? 
A last usertest with one of my interview-partners, helped add some last minute tweaks to the prototype and the overall positive resonance reassured me in my mission.

Final thoughts

The MVP I created serves as a solid foundation with lots of potential to add more features to it. The next steps would be to develop and test the administrator’s perspective, adding a flow for the onboarding process and adding a desktop-version.

This project was a great opportunity to put my knowledge to the test and practise all the methods I learned at the Ironhack UX/UI Bootcamp.
